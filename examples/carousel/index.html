About

To me, photography is not just about taking an image, it's about the thrill of chasing the perfect shot. Whether I am hiking in the wee hours of the morning to get a picture of mist rolling over Hurricane Ridge or standing knee deep in the cold La Jolla surf with my tripod waiting for the sun to set, I try to immerse myself in the elements. And even if I never click the shutter, I never regret trying to explore and fight to see the world through a unique perspective.

As a kid, I had some experience with other art mediums, but photography really had a way of grounding me into nature, people, and reality. I desperately wanted to create memorable images, so I watched countless Youtube videos and spent hours examining Ansel Adams's landscapes of Half Dome and Joel Meyerowitz's scenes of Provincetown. I learned about the importance of exposure, the significance of lines and shapes in composition, and the necessity of seeing things through different perspectives. I took numerous pictures, training my mind to be creative while still maintaining a sense of authenticity. I learned that technique was a means of transforming a scene into a photograph as accurately as possible while recognizing the importance of eliciting a response in the viewer--even if the viewer is just me.

I've also realized that my love for photography, this medium of self-expression, goes beyond the art itself; it's an important connection that I have with the more explorative and curious part of my identity. Every photo I take, whether in the sun-kissed canals of Venice or in my backyard, inspires me to study life, learn, grow, and find some meaning in it all.

As an individual constantly searching for meaning in my life, I try to look beyond what I am familiar with and never neglect the unknown. Traversing new paths and exploring new frontiers imparts a creative perspective in my everlasting journey towards self-actualization. Whether the voyage is through thick rainforests, gravel paths, or misty mountains, I rely on my own curiosity to find what is uncertain. The more places I discover, the closer I get to discovering myself.
